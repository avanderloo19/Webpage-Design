Biography

Benazir Bhutto was born on June 21, 1953, in Karachi, Pakistan. She is the oldest child of the former premier Zulfikar Ali Bhutto. She went on to help found the Pakistan People's Party (PPP) and serve as the nation's first female prime minister (from 1971 to 1977). After completing her education in Pakistan, she pursued her higher education in the United States of America. Bhutto enrolled at Harvard University, where she graduated with a Bachelor of Arts degree in comparative government. She next studied at Oxford University from 1973 to 1977, completing a course in international law and diplomacy.

Zia ul-Haq's dictatorship ended when he was killed in a plane crash in 1988. And Bhutto was elected prime minister three months after giving birth to her first child. Bhutto became the first ever female prime minister of a Muslim nation on December 1, 1988. Bhutto lost the election in 1990. She was sentenced to court, defending herself against many charges of "misconduct while in office" and won a further election in 1993, but was replaced in 1996.

Bhutto was killed when an assassin fired shots while she was in a car. He killed himself and many others in Rawalpindi on December 27, 2007. The attack killed 28 others and wounded another 100. The attacker struck just minutes after Bhutto addressed a rally of thousands of supporters in the garrison city of Rawalpindi, eight miles south of Islamabad.

"I was a very shy girl who led an insulated life; it was only when I came to Oxford, and to Harvard before that, that suddenly I saw the power of people. I didn't know such a power existed, I saw people criticising their own president; you couldn't do that in Pakistan - you'd be thrown in prison." - Benazir Bhutto

Legacy

Benazir Bhutto was born June 21, 1953, in Karachi, Pakistan. Her name means "one without equal."

Bhutto was known to her friends as Pinkie, a childhood nickname given to her by her family because she was an unusually pink baby.

Benazir Bhutton was the first female leader of a Muslim nation in modern History

Her father, Zulfikar Ali Bhutto, was the founder of the Pakistan People's Party. He served as president and then prime minister of Pakistan from 1971 to 1977.
