What to do in Nazca?

The city of Nazca inherited its name from the civilization of the same name from several centuries ago (200 BC-600). The area is known for the famous Nazca lines, lines whose purpose is still not fully known The majority of travelers go there to fly over the lines, but the region has other AMAZING sites to offer!

  • The Nazca Lines
  • Cemetery of Chauchilla
  • Cahuachi
  • Aqueducts of Cantalloc

The Nazca Lines

The Nazca Lines are one of Peru's best-known attractions and yet their mystery remains intact. What is known is that they were created by the Nazca people between 500 BC. and the year 500 AD. Many people do not realize the scale of the site: it is a multitude of geometric drawings and shapes, scattered on a area of 290 square miles!

Cemetery of Chauchilla

The cemetery is one of the most popular attractions outside the Nazca lines. It is a large cemetery of the Inca-Chincha culture , which dates from about the year 900 AC.The cemetery was raided by the huaqueros, "the grave robbers", but the bodies were still well preserved. The well preserved bodies were wrapped in cotton, then covered with resin and kept in adobe graves. They are so well preserved that 1000 years later, some bodies still have hair and skin.

Cahuachi

Another great pre-Inca ceremonial centre Cahuachi, at 24 sq. km, 36 temples, and 36 pyramids, is easily the largest pre-Inca site yet uncovered in Peru. It is a bone-jarring hour ride to get there but this Nasca ceremonial centre is worth the effort. - Tsmada W., Canada

Cahuachi is the most important Nazca center discovered to date. It was a sacred place, where priests and other elite lived, and where visitors came on pilgrimages. It held many religious ceremonies and sacrifices to ensure the protection of the gods.

Aqueducts of Cantalloc

Around one mile south of Nazca are the ruins of Paredones, where remains of an old Inca checkpoint (1471-1493) were found. The site is composed of a central square, administrative buildings, ceremonial spaces, warehouses, watch tower and other constructions.
